## Get started with YBH Pulse Content

This guide walks you through creating an episode, uploading a transcript, generating content, and sharing a brand kit with your guest.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in to Pulse Content">
    Navigate to the login page and sign in with your `@popularit.net` email address.

    <Info>
      If you don't have an account, contact your team administrator. Only `@popularit.net` email addresses are allowed for security.
    </Info>

    Your session will remain active for 24 hours before requiring re-authentication.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new episode">
    From the Episodes page, click **New Episode** in the top-right corner.

    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="With Guest">
        Select a guest from the Guest Directory:
        - Choose an existing guest to link career data and LinkedIn profile
        - Guest status automatically updates as you progress through the workflow
        - Share links can be sent directly to the guest later
      </Tab>
      <Tab title="Without Guest">
        Skip guest selection to create a standalone episode:
        - Add guest details manually via episode metadata
        - Link a guest later from the episode detail page
        - Useful for quick testing or draft episodes
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>

    You'll be redirected to the episode detail page after creation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Upload your transcript">
    On the **Transcript** tab, upload your edited transcript from Adobe Podcast:

    ```text
    Supported formats:
    - Plain text (.txt)
    - Microsoft Word (.docx)
    - PDF documents (.pdf)
    - Drag & drop or paste directly into editor
    ```

    <Note>
      The transcript becomes the **single source of truth** for all generated content. AI fact-checking validates against this transcript to prevent hallucinations.
    </Note>

    ### Auto-format option

    Enable "Auto-format" to clean up transcript formatting:
    - Remove speaker labels (e.g., "Doug:", "Guest:")
    - Fix line breaks and spacing
    - Preserve paragraph structure
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set episode metadata">
    Click **Edit Metadata** to configure:

    - **Episode Number** - Required for share links and file naming
    - **Guest Name** - Used in content generation prompts
    - **Episode Title** - Optional, can be generated with AI
    - **Guest LinkedIn URL** - Enables career timeline generation

    <Tip>
      You can generate episode metadata automatically after PRF is created. Click **Generate Metadata** to create title, descriptions, key takeaways, and show notes based on the transcript.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate PRF document">
    Switch to the **PRF** tab and click **Generate PRF**.

    The AI analyzes your transcript and creates a Podcast Repurposing Framework including:

    - Executive summary of key insights
    - Main themes and discussion topics
    - Quotable soundbites from the guest
    - Business frameworks or methodologies discussed
    - Practical takeaways for IT leaders

    <Warning>
      **Review before approving:** AI-generated content should be fact-checked against the transcript. Use the **Fact Check** feature to validate claims.
    </Warning>

    Edit the PRF using the rich text editor:
    - Format with headings, bold, italics, lists
    - Add tables or highlights for emphasis
    - Use slash commands (`/heading`, `/quote`) for quick formatting

    When satisfied, click **Approve PRF** to enable downstream content generation.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate viral hooks">
    On the **Hooks** tab, click **Generate Hooks**.

    Viral hooks are short, attention-grabbing statements designed for social media:

    <CodeGroup>
      ```text Hook Example 1
      After interviewing 380 IT professionals, one pattern stands out: 
      IT leaders who demand respect before crisis get better results.
      ```

      ```text Hook Example 2
      "The challenge isn't finding a vendor. It's finding the one who 
      sucks the least." - Mark Baker on vendor selection reality.
      ```

      ```text Hook Example 3
      Most CIOs focus on uptime. The best ones focus on why systems 
      go down in the first place.
      ```
    </CodeGroup>

    Review and edit hooks, then **Approve Hooks** to use them in social posts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate social posts">
    Navigate to the **Posts** tab to create platform-specific content.

    ### LinkedIn Posts

    Click **Generate LinkedIn Posts** to create:
    - **Release Day Post** - Announcement when the episode goes live
    - **Follow-Up Post** - Additional insight shared days later

    Both posts include:
    - Verified facts bank (quotes, numbers, frameworks extracted from transcript)
    - Platform-optimized formatting
    - Hashtag suggestions
    - Character counts for LinkedIn's limits

    <Tip>
      Use the **Spell Check** button to run AI grammar and spelling correction on your posts before approving.
    </Tip>

    ### Instagram Captions

    Click **Generate Instagram Posts** for:
    - Story-style captions with hooks
    - Emoji usage for engagement
    - Hashtag recommendations
    - Call-to-action prompts

    Edit captions in the rich text editor and approve when ready.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate visual assets">
    Switch to the **Assets** tab to create infographics and visual content.

    Click **Generate Visual Suggestions** to create 10 suggestions:
    - **4 Data Visualizations** - Charts, frameworks, statistics
    - **4 Cinematic Infographics** - Editorial designs with bold visuals
    - **2 Quote Cards** - Guest quotes with YBH branding

    <Info>
      AI generates complete specs for each suggestion including layout, template, color system, icon style, and image prompt. You can edit prompts before generating images.
    </Info>

    ### Generate images

    For each suggestion:
    1. Review the spec (layout, colors, content breakdown)
    2. Edit the image prompt if needed
    3. Click **Generate Image** to render with Kie.ai
    4. Add YBH logo composite if desired
    5. Approve to add to brand kit

    ### Career timeline

    If guest LinkedIn URL is set, generate a career timeline:
    - Fetches career history from LinkedIn
    - Creates isometric 3D timeline visualization
    - Includes position titles, companies, and dates
    - Automatically added to brand kit
  </Step>

  <Step title="Curate brand kit assets">
    On the **Assets** tab, select which assets to include in the guest's brand kit:

    - Drag to reorder assets (guest sees them in this order)
    - Toggle visibility for each asset
    - Add captions to provide context
    - Preview the guest view with **Preview Brand Kit**

    When satisfied, click **Lock Brand Kit** to finalize curation.

    <Warning>
      Once locked, assets cannot be edited. Unlock to make changes, but note that guests with the share link will see updates immediately.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate and share brand kit link">
    Click **Generate Share Link** to create a unique URL for your guest.

    The share link provides:
    - Episode metadata and guest information
    - PRF summary in an editorial layout
    - Viral hooks with premium typography
    - Career timeline (if available)
    - Visual assets in a masonry grid with lightbox
    - Download buttons for images and full kit ZIP

    ### Share the link

    Copy the link and send it to your guest:

    ```text
    https://pulse.youvebeenheard.com/share/ep-348-mark-baker
    ```

    <Tip>
      Track link views and downloads in the episode analytics dashboard. You'll see when guests access their brand kit and which assets they download.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tips for best results

### Transcript quality

- Use Adobe Podcast transcript as your starting point
- Remove irrelevant content (ads, intros, outros)
- Fix speaker attribution if needed
- Ensure guest name is spelled correctly throughout

### Content review

- Always review AI-generated content before approving
- Run fact-check on suggestions to catch hallucinations
- Use spell check on social posts before scheduling
- Test share links before sending to guests

### Visual assets

- Generate 2-3 rounds of suggestions to get variety
- Edit image prompts to emphasize specific elements
- Add logos to final assets for brand consistency
- Preview assets in brand kit before locking

### Guest experience

- Set episode metadata before generating share link
- Include career timeline if LinkedIn URL available
- Write captions for context on visual assets
- Lock brand kit before sharing to prevent draft content from being visible
